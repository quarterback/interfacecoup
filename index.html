Systems strategy for high-consequence services.

I work with teams navigating scale, complexity, and delivery at the systems layer—where decisions made upstream shape outcomes downstream.

My background spans service design, interaction systems, and scaling design teams in complex environments. Most recently, I led design at 18F, the U.S. government's digital consultancy, where we modernized service delivery for agencies serving millions of users and influenced public sector design globally.

I'm particularly interested in the mechanics of systems—how they break, adapt, and evolve at the point of interaction. This includes studying choice architecture, hype cycles, and the downstream consequences of design decisions in product and platform environments.

Currently, I teach in the Urban Technology program at the University of Michigan and run Neptune Velocity, a studio that helps organizations ship better systems, untangle complexity, and deliver with integrity at scale.
