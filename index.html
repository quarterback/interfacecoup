Most recently, I was Head of Design at 18F, where I led the agency’s design practice. I was responsible for growing and sustaining a multidisciplinary team that shaped how design operated across dozens of high-impact government systems. I am also an incoming Assistant Professor of Practice in Urban Technology at the University of Michigan, where I teach design through the lens of infrastructure, failure, and systems repair.

My work focuses on how public services actually function and break in the real world. I investigate how policy gets operationalized, how harm is embedded into everyday tools, and how we can use design to build resilience into organizations.

Outside of work, I have been a radio news anchor, trivia host, and a state-title winning high school tennis coach. People tend to find me through skeeball, pesäpallo, or that one time we invented a sport. I also co-host the Future Perfect Book Club.
