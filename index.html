Ron Bronson

I'm a designer and strategist working at the intersection of public services, technology, and systems repair. Most recently, I was Head of Design at 18F, where I led the agency's design practice. I was responsible for growing and sustaining a multidisciplinary team that shaped how design operated across dozens of high-impact government systems. I am also an incoming Assistant Professor of Practice in Urban Technology at the University of Michigan.

My academic work explores how public services actually function—and break—in practice. I study how policy gets turned into software, how tools and processes embed harm, and how we might build more resilient institutions through better design. Current areas of focus include digital workflows in public systems, the aesthetics of procedural trust, and the maintenance of legitimacy when core systems stop working.

Outside of work, I've been a radio news anchor, trivia host, and a state-title winning high school tennis coach. People tend to find me through skeeball, pesäpallo, or that one time we invented a sport. I also co-host the Future Perfect Book Club.
