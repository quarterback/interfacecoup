Critical Design & Consequence Theory

I'm Ron Bronson, a critical theorist and design strategist focused on the societal implications of technology systems. I've previously worked at 18F, the federal government's digital services agency, where I led large-scale digital transformation initiatives across multiple federal agencies, as Director & Head of Design for four years.

Since 2017, I've been developing the concept of Consequence Design — a framework for examining how interfaces, services, and systems create cascading effects on social structures and individual lives. My work sits at the intersection of critical theory, service design, and "discursive design" — design that challenges assumptions, provokes debate, and exposes underlying power structures.

Currently, I'm focused on how algorithmic systems and Large Language Models fundamentally reshape human interaction and daily life. This includes exploring the emerging field of Agent Experience, investigating how AI-mediated interactions will be designed, governed, and contextualized in ways that respect human agency and dignity.

My writing on critical design and technological consequences appears in various publications. I'm currently developing a book exploring how interface design shapes socio-political realities and how we might design more just systems.

Teaching & Academic Work

In Spring 2024, I served on the faculty at the University of Michigan's Taubman School of Architecture and Urban Planning, where I taught Service Design & Urban Needs. My course explored how service design methodologies can address complex urban challenges while centering equity and lived experience.

My research and teaching approach draws from critical theory, science and technology studies, and public interest design, with particular attention to how technological systems amplify or mitigate structural inequality.
