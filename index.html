Media Blog

I'm Ron. These days, I'm mostly interested in the mechanics of everyday systems -- think all the screens, sites & forms you deal with on a daily basis -- and how to improve those experiences. Usually, I'm zooming way out and thinking about how to get all the various pieces to operate better together, but other times I'm working in the weeds on solving specific problems within complex organizations around the world.

Skillswise, you can find me where interaction architecture, service design & strategy meet. The joke here is they often don't, due to silos, people's inability to believe that designers can contribute to product delivery except focused on pixels, so I'm usually someone brought in to bridge those gaps. Recently, I organized the Portland Digital Corps, a short-term digital sprint aimed at helping Oregon-based non-profits. I spent the last seven years at 18F.gov, a pioneering digital services agency, the last 4 of those as Head of Design there, leading nearly 40 designers. I'm also an incoming assistant professor of practice in urban technology at the University of Michigan's Taubman College of Architecture & Urban Planning.

While I have deep roots in user-centered design practice and leading agile teams across the delivery lifecycle, I'm most interested these days in thinking aloud about our civil relationship with technology, platforms, software & middleware and what to do about it how it's changing how we live, work and play.

I keep a glossy site with some of my past work. Semi-often, I write blog posts and I co-host a book club podcast with my friend Joel.
