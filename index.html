Speaking Coaching Blog

These days, I'm mostly interested in the mechanics of everyday systems -- think all the screens, sites & forms you deal with on a daily basis -- and how to improve those experiences. Usually, I'm zooming way out and thinking about how to get all the various pieces to operate better together, but other times I'm working in the weeds on solving specific problems within complex organizations around the world.

Skillswise, you can find me where interaction architecture, service design & strategy meet. The joke here is they often don't, due to silos, people's inability to believe that designers can contribute to product delivery except focused on pixels, so I'm usually someone brought in to bridge those gaps. Recently, I organized the Portland Digital Corps, a short-term digital sprint aimed at helping Oregon-based non-profits.

I spent the last seven years at 18F.gov, a pioneering digital services agency, the last 4 of those as Head of Design there, leading nearly 40 designers. I'm also an incoming assistant professor of practice in urban technology in the University of Michigan's Taubman College of Architecture & Urban Planning.

I've spent the last decade as a successful high school tennis coach in my spare time. I usually write my updates on my now-ish page.
