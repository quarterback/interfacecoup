Policy Myopia

Understanding how system design blind spots create cascading impacts across communities.

Policy myopia occurs when system designers fail to see beyond their immediate context, creating cascading impacts that affect diverse communities in unexpected ways. This limited vision creates systemic blind spots that can make systems unusable for many people, regardless of their background or circumstances.

Core Components

Resource Assumptions

Systems often assume universal access to time flexibility, transportation, technology, and social networks. These assumptions create barriers for anyone operating with limited resources.

Infrastructure Requirements

The gap between assumed and actual infrastructure creates cascading difficulties. Missing physical, knowledge, or social infrastructure can make systems effectively unusable.

Compound Effects

Each barrier multiplies difficulty, creating compound effects that drain resources faster while making recovery harder. Options narrow with each additional requirement.

Universal Impact

These patterns affect anyone outside system assumptions: rural communities facing distance barriers, working families managing time constraints, new residents navigating unknown systems.
