Speculative Governance Models

Exploring how technology reshapes governance and imagining alternative futures that might emerge—both naturally and through deliberate design—as traditional systems evolve or collapse.

As technology accelerates beyond policy's ability to regulate it, we're witnessing the emergence of new governance forms—some evolving organically, others being deliberately engineered. This project examines these emerging systems and speculates about potential futures, both dystopian and resilient.

Tesseriocracy: Access as Control

Tesseriocracy represents governance through digital access management, where citizenship becomes a service relationship. Rather than traditional geographic sovereignty, control is exercised through digital permissions and service access. This model draws inspiration from how tech platforms manage millions of users without traditional coercion.

Misistocracy: Technical Control

A malformed evolution of tesseriocracy, misistocracy represents rule by the socially maladapted through technical systems. It combines:

  • Technical competence paired with social incompetence
  • Systematization of human relationships
  • Automated enforcement of artificial social norms
  • Crisis engineering as governance mechanism

The Network State

An emerging form where tech elites create parallel governance structures through:

  • Platform control as governance mechanism
  • Crypto-financial system manipulation
  • Institutional knowledge elimination
  • Parallel system building

The Distributed Branch

A speculative defensive evolution of democratic systems featuring:

  • Distributed authority nodes
  • Random selection with expertise requirements
  • Protected funding mechanisms
  • Rapid response capabilities

Crisis Cascade Framework

Understanding how system failures compound and enable extraction:

  • Strategic degradation of public systems
  • Prevention of community recovery
  • Engineered dependencies
  • Exploitation of collapse

Implementation Patterns

These governance forms manifest through:

  • Digital rights management of citizenship
  • Financial system weaponization
  • Technical complexity as control
  • Process speed exploitation

Resistance Requirements

Defending against these emerging threats requires:

  • Multi-node authority structures
  • Protected funding systems
  • Distributed decision making
  • Knowledge preservation mechanisms

These speculative frameworks help us understand current trends while imagining potential futures and necessary defenses. By examining these emerging patterns, we can better prepare for both challenges and opportunities in governance evolution.
