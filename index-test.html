Ron Bronson

I work on public systems—designing, maintaining, and diagnosing the parts people rely on but rarely see. My practice blends service design, interaction infrastructure, policy mechanics, and institutional repair.

I spent seven years at 18F helping federal agencies build and improve digital services, leading design strategy and advising executive teams. Most recently, I organized the Portland Digital Corps, a civic sprint pairing technologists with nonprofits on urgent projects.

In 2024, I led early explorations into LLM observability and trust tooling—scoring agents, flagging failure states, and designing the seams between model behavior and real-world consequence. That work’s paused, but the questions aren’t.

I teach urban technology at the University of Michigan. I also run a research initiative called the Institute for Public Mechanics, focused on capacity-building for service delivery and interaction integrity across government and infrastructure.
