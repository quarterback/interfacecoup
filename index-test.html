Speaking Now-ish Blog

Design for the speculative present.

I work on systems—how they’re built, how they fail, and how we might repair them. I’m a designer, strategist, and sometimes educator focused on public infrastructure, civic tech, and large-scale service transformation. That’s included everything from leading federal design teams to managing government websites, running content migrations, and helping institutions make sense of the services they offer.

Even after years in leadership, I still get excited by IA puzzles and the messy details of content strategy. I’ve worked inside agencies, city governments, federal programs, and academic institutions—and I tend to ask inconvenient questions about how things actually work.

You might know me from my work on Consequence Design, or from running civic events in the Pacific Northwest or from teaching at the University of Michigan.
